Ocicat

This feline breed boasts a medium to large stature, characterized by a robust and muscular physique, underpinned by a sturdy, dense skeletal framework.

Personality

Exceptionally intelligent, dynamic, sociable, and people-centric, the Ocicat may not bear the size of a big cat, but they certainly possess the personality and charisma of one! According to their owners, they exhibit dog-like qualities and relish engaging with family members and guests. They have a habit of shadowing people throughout the house, overseeing and sometimes even meddling in their activities. Their remarkable athleticism allows them to execute impressive high jumps, maintain balance on precarious ledges, and reach spots that most other cats wouldn't consider.

Generally Healthy Breed +

Ocicats, like other domestic cat breeds, can be prone to certain health issues. However, they are generally considered a healthy breed. Some potential health concerns that may affect Ocicats, as well as other cat breeds, include:

  • Periodontal Disease: Dental issues can be a problem for cats, and Ocicats are no exception. Regular dental care is essential to prevent tooth and gum problems.
  • Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease (FLUTD): This condition can affect cats, causing issues like urinary tract infections and urinary crystals. It's important to provide fresh water and a balanced diet to help prevent this condition.

History and Origins +

The Ocicat came into existence unintentionally in 1964 when a breeder aimed to develop a cat resembling a Siamese cat with the ticked point pattern of an Abyssinian. To their surprise, the breeding attempts yielded a striking spotted kitten that bore a remarkable resemblance to a wild Ocelot, thus inspiring the name "Ocicat." This kitten served as the cornerstone for breeding programs that combined Siamese, Abyssinian, and American Shorthair genetics, ultimately resulting in the establishment of the Ocicat breed. The first Ocicats made their way to the United Kingdom in the late 1980s.

Outdoors or Indoors +

Providing a spacious and highly secure cat enclosure is essential for the Ocicat unless you can effectively cat-proof your entire garden. These cats require ample room to exercise, explore, and showcase their agility. It's highly likely that you'll also require a secure area to leave your Ocicat when you're not at home, unless your entire residence is cat-proofed.
Unsupervised outdoor access is not advisable for Ocicats. While they are bold and athletic, their curious nature, size, and penchant for getting into mischief make them susceptible to potential risks and theft.

Enrichment and Specific Needs +

The Ocicat is likely to be just as demanding as a dog when it comes to attention, mental stimulation, training, and play. Clicker training can be highly effective in teaching your cat useful behaviors, such as guiding them to a specific bed or marker, getting them into a cat carrier, keeping them off designated surfaces, and showcasing entertaining tricks that highlight their agility.
In addition to training, it's advisable to feed most of their meals through puzzle toys that engage their hunting, searching, and climbing instincts, as these activities help them find, retrieve, and savor their food. When accommodating an Ocicat in your home, consider the layout of your furniture and decor, as they might push knick-knacks from shelves, shred paperwork, and even pilfer and conceal shiny objects.

Nutrition and Feeding +

Each cat possesses its own unique preferences, dislikes, and dietary requirements. However, it's important to remember that all cats are carnivores and must acquire 41 distinct and essential nutrients from their diet. The specific proportions of these nutrients can vary based on factors like age, lifestyle, and overall health. Consequently, it's logical that a growing, high-energy kitten will require a different nutrient balance in her diet compared to a less active senior cat.

Grooming +

Make it a weekly routine to brush or comb your Ocicat. This not only maintains their coat but also offers valuable bonding time and fosters good handling habits. A greasy coat can be an indicator that your cat is not engaging in self-grooming, which could be a sign of underlying health issues.
As is the case with all cats, it is advisable to stay on top of regular vaccinations and parasite control.

Suitability for Family Life +

Despite their relatively moderate appearance, Ocicats are known for their often strikingly intense behavior. They thrive in environments that are securely and suitably Ocicat-proofed. Owners should be prepared to engage and entertain these robust, opinionated felines. They are better suited to tranquil, low-key households with older teenagers and plenty of room to accommodate their active nature.
