Balinese Cat

The Balinese cat is a medium-sized feline known for its graceful, slender, and elegant appearance. It boasts a fine-boned build and sports a semi-long, silky coat. Unlike many other long-haired breeds, the Balinese coat lacks an undercoat, resulting in a sleek, close-fitting, non-bulky fur that is far less prone to matting.

Personality

The Balinese cat is a playful and inquisitive feline, known for its clever and demanding nature. It thrives on attention, fun, and engaging games, but it may exhibit loud and destructive behavior when lacking these stimuli. Consider this breed to be on the higher-maintenance side of the feline scale, making them more of a specialist cat rather than a tranquil, quiet companion.

History and Origins +

The Balinese cat owes its origin to a chance occurrence of long-haired kittens within Siamese cat litters. These long-haired kittens were initially noticed in the 1940s. American breeders made a deliberate effort to segregate and mate these kittens in an attempt to consistently produce long-haired cats with the distinctive Siamese appearance. Notably, the Balinese breed has no ancestral connection with any other cat breed, and its introduction to the UK occurred in 1973.

Outdoors or Indoors +

The Balinese cat's remarkable characteristics and vocal, insistent meowing make them less suitable for unsupervised outdoor excursions, as they may bother neighbors, engage in playful antics, or become targets for theft. However, given their athleticism and intelligence, they benefit from the stimulation and enrichment of the outdoors. To meet their needs, we suggest a secure garden that's cat-proofed or an expansive enclosure, often referred to as a "catio." These options provide your Balinese with ample space to play, observe birds, and indulge in climbing activities.

Enrichment and Specific Needs +

Similar to their Siamese predecessors, Balinese cats have a penchant for climbing, exploration, and play. It's wise to invest in expansive cat trees and a variety of toys to keep them engaged. Otherwise, you'll likely find them diverting their attention to your delicate decorations and furniture. In fact, if you own a Balinese cat, it's advisable to limit the presence of fragile ornaments and trinkets, as they may frequently end up on the floor!

While these felines can amuse themselves with jungle-gyms and interactive food-dispensing toys, it's essential to remember that nothing truly substitutes the time spent with you. They are quite insistent on this front, so be prepared to engage with your Balinese. Explore our suggestions for entertaining cat games you can enjoy together.

Nutrition and Feeding +

Each cat is unique, with their own individual preferences, dislikes, and dietary requirements. However, it's important to remember that cats are obligate carnivores, meaning they must derive 41 specific nutrients from their food. The exact proportion of these nutrients can vary based on factors such as age, lifestyle, and overall health. Therefore, it's not surprising that a lively, growing kitten will need a different nutrient balance in her diet compared to a more sedentary senior cat.

Additional considerations include ensuring you provide the right quantity of food to maintain an "ideal body condition," as outlined in feeding guidelines. Grooming +

The Balinese cat, with its exquisite yet delicate coat, requires consistent and frequent grooming to ensure it remains in excellent condition and free from mats and tangles. It's essential to note that a greasy coat may signal that your cat has ceased self-grooming, which can be a sign of declining health. Just like all cats, the Balinese greatly benefits from regular vaccinations, effective feline parasite control, and an annual veterinary health check to ensure their well-being.

Suitability for Family Life +

If you're prepared to dedicate a significant portion of your leisure time to entertain your cat, and you don't mind parting ways with knick-knacks, trinkets, and ornaments from your windowsills and mantelpieces, the Balinese might be the right choice for you! This breed is likely better suited for an adult-only household or one with older children who can understand and respect a cat's personal space and needs, as the Balinese can be quite vocal and demanding as a companion.
